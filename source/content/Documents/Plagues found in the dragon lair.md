- [[Alley Plague]]

Consulted books only describe the [[alley plague]] in name. it seems to infect through the air, especially in the vicinity of bodies. It impairs blood clotting and creates pimples and rashes. It does not share symptoms with the victim.
[^1]

- [[Crimson Death]]

Literature on this plague was not available beyond it's name. Testing shows a subject bleeding to death (usually internal) and from that blood forms a mist that (presumably) seeks other victims. These symptoms are most similar to the victim's, work on a cure for this disease is a priority.
[^2]

- [[Empire Plague]]

Literature was non-existent beyond the name (known from ancestral records of long-lived races). It is highly contagious, and the fastest spreading disease I have so far researched. The plague cases red and bleeding eyes, as well as bleeding purple sores. It seems to be transferred through the air.

- [[Feather Lung]]

A rather well-known contagious disease that plagued Waterdeep 18 years ago. It is spread by inhalation and takes 1 to three days to take effect. It destroys the lungs, shriveling them, which eventually results in asphyxiation. 
The victim cannot have [[Feather Lung]] as symptoms did not include coughing or anything to do with the lungs. 

- [[Infernal Death]]

Only described in name, the [[Infernal Death]] is the most vicious of plagues researched so far. It is transferred through skin contact. It starts with mania and fever, and can be recognised by heightened color in the eyes, hair, and skin. Death occurs rather suddenly, and with it comes an internal reaction that results in combustion. It does not share symptoms with the victim.

- [[Leper's Curse]]

Literature does not describe the curse beyond it's name. It is transferred through fluids. Initial effects show absence of pain and eventually all sensation, and then all senses. It might cause insanity before death. It does not share symptoms with the victim.

- [[Panicked Plague]]

Only the name and cure are described in literature. It is transferred through skin contact, and turns the skin lightly green. It covers it in welts and abcesses, and tends to kill in a day. Literature describes that mundane and magical treatment do not have effect. It does not share symptoms with the victim 

- [[Saprophytic Plague]]

Much more is known about the [[Saprophytic plague]] in the literature that I've consulted. It is a recent plague that is not very viable on its own, Since it relied on the existence of its creator, a 
'Lichen' Lich therefore samples of it have become very unstable. To even be able to reproduce it is a challenge. It could not have survived in the victim, it would have fizzled before it could cause real damage. The effects of it were a fever, oozing lesions and transformation into a mindless zombie. It was acquired through breathing in spores, and victims of the disease would spread more spores. Fungi are immune to the disease.

- [[Shaking Plague]]

This plague ravaged Scardale a little over a century ago. It is suspected to be magical and physical in nature. It is spread through physical contact, though only in the first stages. It has effects such as tremors, convulsions, low body temperature, sweating, pale skin, pox and lack of speech. The victim does not share symptoms with this plague.
- [[Spider Plague]]

An older plague that does not have much information written about it. It seems to be less of a plague and more a specific species of spiders. It is spread by contact. Sores start to slowly cover the body, and these sores are spider eggs. Once these hatch they start to consume the subject. The victim cannot have this plague as the symptoms don't match.

- [[Spotted Plague]]

Called one of the most dangerous non-magical diseases, it is supposedly worse than [[Smallpox]] or [[typhus]]. It had an outbreak in Iriaebor longer than a century ago, and so still has literature from that time. It is Spread by skin-to-skin contact and takes effect in a day. First the skin takes on a light pink, then breaks out in white pustules which leave behind scars. It rots away almost all kinds of tissue, causes loss of balance, reasoning, sense of spatial awareness, smell and sometimes vision. It usually kills in the first 24 hours. It does not share symptoms with the victim.

- [[Consecrated Mists]]

Research was requested and paid for by [[Garro Skann|G. Skann]]. This plague does not seem to have symptoms that the victim shares. It is a magical plague and seems to have almost a mind of it's own. It functions like a quickly spreading mist, and drains the life of the creatures it consumes. The consumption is not unlike necromantic tissue damage, the body tissue experiences necrosis.
